# The Royal Enfield Himalayan 450

The Royal Enfield Himalayan is an iconic adventure tourer that first debuted in 2016. While the original 411 was known for its rugged, go-anywhere nature, the new 450 is a ground-up evolution. Named after the toughest mountain range in the world, this motorcycle is designed to bridge the gap between a daily commuter and a hardcore off-roader.

In India, the Himalayan 450 starts at a base price of 2.85 Lakhs and goes up to 2.98 Lakhs for the top-end Hanle Black variant.

#### Performance and the Sherpa Engine

The Himalayan 450 features the all-new "Sherpa" engine, which is Royal Enfield’s first-ever liquid-cooled motor. This 452cc single-cylinder engine puts out 40 HP and 40 NM of Torque at its peak. The bike delivers a fuel economy of roughly 28–30 km per liter, which is acceptable for a high-performance adventure tourer. The engine is mated to a 6-speed gearbox with a slip-and-assist clutch for smoother transitions.

#### How is the Himalayan Like to Drive??

On open highways, the new Himalayan feels exceptionally planted. The liquid-cooled engine allows for a much higher cruising speed compared to its predecessor. When you need to overtake on the highway, the mid-range torque ensures the bike pulls cleanly without vibration. 

The motorcycle features 2 riding modes: Performance and Eco. These modes alter the throttle response and can even toggle the rear ABS off for off-road use. The Showa upside-down forks and linkage-type rear mono-shock provide a "plush" ride that absorbs almost any undulation on Indian roads.

#### Infotainment and Technology

The Himalayan 450 comes with several modern features, including a world-first circular 4-inch TFT display. This system allows for full-map navigation via Google Maps, Bluetooth connectivity for music and calls, and an integrated USB-C charging port. The bike also features all-LED lighting and a joystick controller on the switchgear for easy menu navigation.

#### Comfort and Ergonomics

The interior "cabin" of this motorcycle—the rider's cockpit—is designed for long-distance endurance. The split-seat setup is well-contoured, and the 825mm seat height (adjustable to 845mm) ensures a commanding view of the road. 

The fuel tank capacity is 17 litres, which provides a significant range for touring. The rear rack is ready for luggage, and the bike supports an array of official accessories like panniers and top boxes for carrying gear on cross-country trips.

#### Pros and Cons of the Himalayan

* Excellent suspension travel for Indian potholes
* Modern liquid-cooled engine with great highway pace
* Industry-leading Google Maps integration
* The seat can feel a bit firm on 500km+ ride days
* High-speed vibrations felt in the footpegs at 6,000 RPM
* Heavy weight makes it difficult to maneuver in tight parking spots

#### Final Verdict

If you are looking for a versatile, "do-it-all" adventure bike that can handle both the office commute and the Leh-Ladakh circuit, we reckon you should definitely buy the Himalayan. However, if you are strictly looking for a lightweight city bike, the sheer size and weight of the 450 might be overkill.
