# The Triumph Speed 400

The Triumph Speed 400 is a modern-classic roadster that brings the iconic British heritage to a more accessible segment. Launched in India through a partnership with Bajaj, this bike follows the design language of the larger Speed Twin 900 and 1200. It is often seen as a "gateway" into the premium motorcycling world.

The Speed 400 starts with an attractive price point of 2.34 Lakhs (Ex-showroom), making it a highly competitive offering in the 400cc class.

#### Performance and the TR-Series Engine

The Speed 400 comes with a 398cc liquid-cooled, DOHC engine known as the TR-Series. This motor puts out 40 PS of power along with 37.5 NM of Torque. The bike gives a mileage of around 28–32 km per liter, which is quite impressive for a bike with this much punch. It is driven by a 6-speed gearbox that feels crisp and precise.

#### How is the Speed 400 Like to Drive??

The Speed 400 feels lovely to drive in urban environments. The engine is extremely smooth, and the torque is spread evenly across the rev range. In the city, you don't need to shift gears constantly as the bike has great "tractability." 

On the highway, it reaches 100 kmp/h effortlessly and can top out at 145 kmp/h. The steering weight is light, making it very agile in corners. It features a Ride-by-Wire throttle which ensures that the power delivery is instantaneous and smooth. The bike also gets a Switchable Traction Control system, which helps in maintaining stability during wet or slippery conditions.

#### Infotainment and Features

The Speed 400 features a semi-digital instrument cluster that combines an analogue speedometer with a small LCD screen. Features include a gear position indicator, fuel gauge, and a clock. It also comes with a hidden USB-C charging port near the handlebar, premium LED lighting with a signature DRL, and an immobilizer for added security.

#### Comfort and Quality

Jumping onto the seat of the Speed 400, the bike feels remarkably high-quality. The paint finish and the gold-anodized forks give it a premium look. The seating position is upright and neutral, which helps the rider feel relaxed during city commutes. 

The seat height is a very accessible 790mm, which is perfect for Indian riders. While the bike is compact, the tank grips and footpeg positioning allow for a very "connected" feel with the machine. 

#### Pros and Cons of the Speed 400

* Top-tier build quality and premium finish
* Very smooth and refined engine performance
* Lightweight and easy to handle in traffic
* The bike feels a bit small for taller riders
* Radiator fan can be loud and blows heat on legs in traffic
* The instrument cluster lacks turn-by-turn navigation

#### Final Verdict

If you are looking for a sophisticated, fast, and beautifully built roadster for your daily city runs and occasional weekend trips, the Speed 400 is an excellent choice. However, if you plan on doing heavy touring with a pillion and a lot of luggage, you might find the compact dimensions a bit limiting.
